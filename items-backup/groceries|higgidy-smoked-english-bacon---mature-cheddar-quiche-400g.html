Information

Description

Seeded shortcrust pastry crammed with mature cheddar and sautéed onions in a creamy free range egg filling, topped with smoked English bacon lardons.

The best food always looks a bit Higgidy

We've added a little British twist to this French classic. Perfect enjoyed on a leafy Parisian boulevard, but just as delicious eaten in an English cul-de-sac.

We've been making delicious pies, quiches and sausage rolls from our kitchen in Shoreham-by-Sea since 2003. Baked in small batches and using ingredients just like you'd find in your own kitchen at home, we don't mind if each one of our pies, quiches and sausage rolls doesn't look quite like its neighbour. In fact, we think that the best food always looks a bit Higgidy.

Preparation

Raw Courgettes
Grate green and yellow courgettes, add freshly crushed garlic, a splash of olive oil and lemon juice, season generously and serve.

Oven cook: From Chilled

This quiche is ready to eat cold but tastes best heated up. Preheat oven to 180°C/350°F/Gas Mark 4. Remove all packaging & place on a tray on the middle shelf of the oven for about 25 minutes. This is a guide only (some ovens vary). Do not reheat once cooled.

Storage

Keep your quiche refrigerated below 5°C. If freezing, freeze before use by date. Use within one month and once defrosted eat within 24 hours. Once thawed, don't re-freeze. Always defrost fully before cooking.

Gimme More of this superb quiche
For non-greedy people, this quiche does six normal sized portions, either with veg or salad, or to put into a packed lunchbox, or to portion up and have at those Friday afternoon office parties (oh how I miss those).

I am drooling as I write this review.
Yes, I recommend this product.
