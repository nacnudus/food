During cooking: Stir halfway through After cooking: Leave to stand for 1 minute. Do not reheat once cooled. All cooking appliances vary. This is a guide only. Microwave on full power. I only tried this because of the reviews!
I've been disappointed before by grainy, watery, wallpaper paste type frozen mash in the past, and as the title says, I only tried this because of the reviews! I thought it sounded too good to be true, but I found myself pleasantly surprised for a change.
Whilst I wouldn't say this is as good as home made from scratch it's not far off. It's excellent for a frozen product. Better by far than any other frozen mash product I've tried. So if you're too short of time or inclination, this is a worthy substitute for the real deal.
After 'nuking' it's easy to make any additions you want, like grated cheese, fresh herbs or additional seasoning. Well worth keeping some in the freezer on standby. :-)
I like to add some fine ground white pepper to perk it up - as recommended by the Hairy Bikers - it gives a nice flavour lift without turning your mash grey like black pepper would.
Tip: If you plan to use this to top a Cottage/Shepherds/Fish pie it's way easier to spread warm mash over a base mix that has been allowed to cool and then chilled. After topping the pie give the mash a good going over with a fork to create a rough texture that will crisp up in the oven and make a nice crispy/crunchy skin on the pie.
Yes, I recommend this product.
