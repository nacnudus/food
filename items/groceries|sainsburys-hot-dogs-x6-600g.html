Preparation and Safety

Cooking instructions: other

To pan fry from chilled: Before Cooking: Heat 15ml of vegetable oil in a frying pan. Remove all packaging and when the oil is hot add the hot dogs to the frying pan. During cooking: Reduce heat and turn frequently. After cooking: Check food is piping hot and no pink colour remains. All cooking appliances vary. This is a guide only. Moderate/low heat: 18 mins

Warnings:

Microbiological Safety: Check food is cooked thoroughly before serving

Storage

For use by date: see front of pack. Keep refrigerated, once opened consume within 2 days and do not exceed the use by date.

For best Quality, freeze as soon as possible after purchase and always within the use by date. Once frozen consume within 1 month. Once defrosted use the same day.

Do not refreeze once defrosted.

WORST HOTDOGS EVER
These are probably the worst hotdogs I have ever tried to eat.

Actually they take longer, but 18 minutes is the recommended time! Normally, you heat a hotdog in simmering water... The result of frying them (the only method listed in the instructions) is that the skins are crisped where a hotdog has a "clean" skin. Of course, this would not be so bad if they were better sausages. At least, you could say, "well, they aren't hot dogs but..." But nothing; they aren't hotdogs and they are terrible sausages.
Better hotdogs/franks are from Herta (Sainsburys) or Bavarian franks from a supposedly more "upmarket" supermarket.
Never buy these hotdogs. We threw them away.

Very good indeed
A pleasant find. These sausages are thick, tasty and very filling. They swell considerably in the frying pan. Excellent with horseradish sauce, mayonnaise, mustard and/or relish! While they have a good flavour, they are not spicy - some peppers really brought out the taste. The first time we made them, we browned them for around 10 minutes, then stewed them for another 10 minutes in a lovely gravy made with chopped leeks, puréed tomatoes and crème fraîche. Much better than "hot dog" frankfurters.
The only downside is that the meat content is only 82%. The sausages would have been tastier if this had been higher. We'll keep this product on our Favourites list as long as Sainsbury's keeps it gluten-free.
Yes, I recommend this product.
