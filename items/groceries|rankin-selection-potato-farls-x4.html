Disappointing
Farl is any quadrant-shaped flatbread, that's cut into 4 pieces (farl means fourths). It's a traditional Irish Potato Bread made from wheat. In Northern Ireland, the term refers to soda bread, and also to potato bread or cakes (potato farls)
Farl is made by spreading dough in a rough, circular shape, on a griddle or frying pan. The circle is cut into 4 equal pieces and cooked on both sides. It should have a soft texture & a bland flavour. For a denser & moister farl, uses less flour & bicarbonate of soda. For a light & fluffy, more bread-like farl, use more flour & more bicarbonate of soda & milk
Now onto Paul Rankin's product; advertising states that if you toast these rectangular-shaped farls the outside of them should be crispy, and the inside smooth yet textured. Potato farls are traditionally a breakfast food, and as they're bland, make a good blank canvas to add any flavours of your choosing. Common additions include: simply just butter/margerine, melted cheese, fried mushrooms, sausages, poached eggs, boiled egg, fried egg, bacon, smoked salmon, baked beans, or jam, or any other breakfast foods, tho anything can be added without limitations of only breakfast items
Make it sound like a Gaelic little pleasure don't I? Well it should be, however, there's not much to this product, it barely exists! The little that's there is quite dry and lifeless. Yes, bland is expected, but from Paul Rankin one doesn't expect poor ingredients. The 90% reconstituted dried potato flakes are invisible, and yet advertising states "..packed full with Irish potato.."
Disappointing. That's why we made our own and the results were polar opposite!
No, I do not recommend this product.
